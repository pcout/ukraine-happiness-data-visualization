War and Wellbeing in Ukraine

Is it possible to be happy with war raging all around?
What does happiness even mean for a country that has been living at the epicenter of geopolitical turmoil for several years?

What is the data telling us ?

The most striking insight is that happiness scores actually increased immediately following the full-scale invasion in 2022, contradicting the intuitive expectation that war leads to immediate despair. In 2021 (pre-invasion), Ukraine's Happiness Score was 4.87. In 2022 (invasion year), it rose to 5.08. A +4.3% increase in the happiness index during the first year of the full-scale war.

Let's break it down

Generosity scores skyrocketed from 0.33 in 2021 to 0.71 in 2024.
A massive +115% increase in generosity over three years. This jump represents the millions of Ukrainians who mobilized to volunteer, donate to the army, and host displaced neighbors. While 2022 showed resilience, the data for 2024 indicates that the psychological burden is taking its toll. By 2024, data shows a decrease in those identifying as "happy" - dropping to 58% - attributed to economic pressures, separation from families, and the prolonged nature of the conflict.

Can you see ?

When Ukrainians felt they had agency and control over their lives, their happiness rose, regardless of the economic situation. This freedom metric surged during the war years. This suggests that the fight for national independence gave people a profound sense of existential freedom and purpose, which sustained their morale even as physical restrictions increased.

Generosity, Freedom, and Corruption. These three parameters moved in a distinct pattern during the war years (2022-2024). As people felt more "free", they became more generous. This is visible in the volunteerism boom. The data shows a moderate positive link between Perception of Corruption and Happiness. This is unusual but likely reflects the wartime reality where corruption scandals became more visible (higher perception) because the public demanded higher accountability from leaders during the war, coincident with the period of high national unity.

Ukraine in the Europe context

Ukraine occupies a unique position. While its economic and happiness metrics often align with the "worst" ranked countries, it demonstrates world-class resilience in social metrics, outperforming even the "best" countries in Generosity. Over the full 2015-2024 period, Ukraine's median happiness (4.62) is slightly lower than the median of the "worst" group (4.80). However, this score notably trended upward in 2022 despite the invasion, showing a defiance of these long-term averages.

How does Ukraine compares

Despite the war, Ukrainians feel a level of social support that is much closer to the world's happiest nations than to the struggling ones. Ukraine sits roughly 70% of the way toward the "best" standard. This suggests that interpersonal networks and community aid are functioning at a near-Western European level, acting as a crucial buffer against the trauma of war.
